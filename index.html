Portfolio Observations
Geographic concentration. The portfolio is anchored by China, India and Brazil, which together account for over four-fifths of NDB commitments; Russia, South Africa and Bangladesh make up the remainder.
Sector profile. Transport is the single largest sector at roughly a third of all projects, led by China and India. Digital infrastructure is represented by a single project across the entire portfolio.
2020 commitment spike. The peak in 2020 commitments corresponds to COVID-19 emergency assistance facilities, which moved from concept to approval in one to two months and pulled that year's average approval time well below trend.
Lending model. Sovereign transactions make up close to three-quarters of the portfolio. Non-Sovereign lending is concentrated in India, and crossed half of all approvals for the first time in 2025.
Currency composition. Local-currency lending is almost entirely Renminbi. Outside China, the portfolio is overwhelmingly hard-currency, with only a handful of Rand and a single Rupee transaction.
Membership expansion. Among members admitted since the founding five, only Bangladesh (joined 2021) has an active pipeline so far, and it is entirely climate-aligned. The UAE (2021), Egypt (2023) and Algeria (2025) have been admitted but are yet to be allotted any projects in the portfolio; Colombia and Uzbekistan (2025) and prospective member Uruguay are the most recent additions.
Approval velocity. Median concept-to-approval time sits well below the 18–24 month range typical of larger multilateral lenders, with Non-Sovereign deals clearing materially faster than Sovereign ones.
